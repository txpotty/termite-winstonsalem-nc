Discarded Termite Wings

Termite 'swarmers' are reproductive termites that leave the colony to establish new nests. After swarming (typically in the spring or after rainfall), they shed their wings, often found near windowsills, doorways, or light fixtures. The presence of discarded wings, which look similar to ant wings but are equal in length, is a strong indicator that a mature termite colony is located nearby. Seeing discarded wings means a termite swarm has occurred, and it's highly likely that an infestation is active.
